# AI Friday — Voice & Tone

## Who we are
A small group of people in New Orleans who meet on Friday mornings
to talk about AI. Mostly startup founders and operators. Some are
building with AI every day. Some are just getting started. Everyone's
trying to keep up.

## Who we're talking to
Someone who runs a startup or works at one. They use ChatGPT, maybe
Claude, probably a handful of AI tools for work. They're not training
models. They're making decisions — what to build, what to buy, where
to invest their time. They feel like things are moving fast and they
might be falling behind. They're right that things are moving fast.
They're wrong that they're behind.

## How we sound
- Like a friend who reads a lot and shares the good stuff.
- Direct. Say what something is, not what it "represents" or "signals."
- Opinions stated flat. No hedging with "arguably" or "it's worth noting."
- Dry over cute. Funny without trying to be funny.
- Specific. Real names. "Simon Willison wrote" not "one notable blogger."
- Honest. "I don't know what to make of this yet" is fine.
- Warm but not performative. New Orleans energy — unhurried, genuine, no pretense.
- Short when short is right. Longer when the thing deserves it.

## How we DON'T sound
- Not a SaaS landing page. We're not selling anything.
- Not a conference. No "thought leadership," no panels, no keynotes.
- Not breathless. Nothing is "game-changing" or "revolutionary."
- Not defensive. Don't define ourselves by what we're not.
- Not a tech bro meetup. No hype cycles, no FOMO manufacturing.
- Not corporate. No "ecosystem," "leverage," "harness," "robust."

## Hard rules — never do these
- No "here's the thing" / "here's the kicker" / "here's where it gets interesting"
- No "It's not X — it's Y" negative parallelism
- No "The result? Devastating." self-posed rhetorical questions
- No "imagine a world where"
- No "let's break this down" / "let's unpack" / "let's dive in" / "let's delve"
- No "think of it as" patronizing analogies
- No "from X to Y" false ranges
- No "serves as" / "stands as" / "marks a pivotal moment"
- No "rich tapestry" / "landscape" / "ecosystem" / "paradigm shift"
- No "quietly" / "deeply" / "fundamentally" / "remarkably"
- No tricolon abuse (three parallel things in a row, repeatedly)
- No anaphora abuse (same sentence opening repeated)
- No "It's worth noting" / "Importantly" / "Interestingly" / "Notably"
- No stakes inflation ("will reshape how we think about everything")
- No bold-first bullet points (every bullet starting with **Bold Thing**)
- No fractal summaries (intro that previews, body, conclusion that restates)
- No excessive em-dashes (max 2-3 per page, only where natural)
- No "game-changer" / "powerful" / "streamline" / "leverage" / "harness"
- No short punchy fragments for manufactured emphasis ("And it worked. Period.")
- No historical analogy stacking ("Just like Apple... and Uber... and Airbnb...")
- No invented concept labels ("the supervision paradox")
- No value-prop card patterns (three cards with bold headers = SaaS landing page)

## What good sounds like

### Website / landing page
Should sound like you're explaining the group to someone at a coffee shop.
Not pitching. Not selling. Just telling them what it is and when it meets.
If they're interested, great. If not, that's fine too.

### Daily brief
- Daring Fireball: blockquotes from source, then a direct opinion stated flat.
- Simon Willison: dense links, specific technical observations, "I tried this."
- A good group chat: "did you see this?" + one honest reaction.

### Slack
Just people talking. No brand voice in Slack. The bot posts briefs;
everything else is humans being humans.

## The feeling
You walked into a room on a Friday morning. There's coffee. People
have laptops open. Someone's showing something they built over the
weekend. Someone else has a question about which tool to use for
something. It's small, it's useful, and you leave feeling like you
learned something. That's it.
