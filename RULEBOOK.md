# AI Friday Daily Brief — Rulebook

## Mission
Curate a focused, high-signal daily brief for the AI Friday NOLA Slack.
Help busy builders stay current without drowning in noise.

## What Makes the Cut

### ✅ INCLUDE
- **New AI tool launches** — especially ones people can try today
- **Major model releases** — new models from OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Meta, Mistral, open-source community
- **Significant capability jumps** — benchmarks, new abilities, multimodal breakthroughs
- **API & platform changes** — pricing changes, new endpoints, deprecations
- **Open-source releases** — new libraries, frameworks, model weights
- **Regulation & policy** — only major moves (executive orders, EU AI Act milestones, major lawsuits)
- **Local/NOLA relevance** — anything touching New Orleans, Louisiana, or Gulf South tech scene
- **Practical tutorials** — that teach something actionable in <30 min
- **Funding rounds >$50M** — signals where the industry is heading

### ❌ EXCLUDE
- Hype pieces with no substance ("AI will change everything")
- Listicles rehashing old tools
- Paywalled content with no free summary
- Drama / Twitter beefs / personality worship
- Incremental updates (minor version bumps, small bug fixes)
- Rumor-only stories with no credible sourcing
- Crypto/Web3 projects that slapped "AI" on the label

## Formatting Rules

### Daily Brief Structure
```
🌞 AI Friday Daily Brief — {date}

💥 Big Moves (0-3 items)
Headline stories everyone needs to know

🛠️ Tools & Releases (2-5 items)
New things you can use right now

📚 Worth Reading (1-3 items)
Deeper pieces for the curious

🎯 NOLA Spotlight (0-1 items)
Local relevance, if any
```

### Per-Item Format
- **Bold headline** with link
- 1-2 sentence summary in plain language
- Why it matters (one line)
- Tag: `[Tool]` `[Model]` `[Paper]` `[Policy]` `[Open Source]` `[Tutorial]` `[Funding]`

## Scoring (internal, not shown to users)
Each candidate item gets scored 0-10:
- Novelty (0-3): Is this actually new?
- Impact (0-3): How many people does this affect?
- Actionability (0-2): Can someone do something with this today?
- Credibility (0-2): Is the source reliable?

**Threshold: 5+ to include.** Max 12 items per daily brief.

## Tone
- Conversational but not sloppy
- Opinionated when warranted (“this is a big deal” / “meh”)
- No corporate speak
- Emoji are fine, don't overdo it
- Write for builders, not executives

## Cadence
- **Daily** at **7:00 AM CT** (Central Time, New Orleans)
- No brief on weekends unless something major drops
- Weekend recap on Monday if needed

## Feedback Loop
- Track Slack reactions (🔥 = great, 👎 = miss)
- Weekly self-review: which items got engagement?
- Adjust source weights based on hit rate
